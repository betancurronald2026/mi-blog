Critical Thinking & Media Literacy

Using Critical Thinking To Avoid Manipulation

📅 April 13, 2026

Critical thinking is the ability to evaluate a situation carefully using logic, data, and multiple opinions instead of believing the first thing you hear or the first thought that comes to your mind. On the other hand, media literacy is the ability to consume media content using critical thinking.

The media are always trying to manipulate you to make you buy something, to radicalize you, or to keep your attention as much as possible. Software apps are not the exception; the information and posts that social media decides to show you are not random but are selected by an algorithm that knows what you might like and tries to keep you hooked for hours.

That is not bad in itself; the problem is that you can easily become addicted due to an algorithm that was made to analyze your behavior and whose ultimate goal is to prevent you from leaving the app.

Another problem is that you can end up in an echo chamber, where you only watch posts and read comments that are aligned with your beliefs, or even that depict people who think differently from you as your enemy. That happens because the app wants to make you happy and comfortable, and to achieve that, it tries to make you feel like you are always right.

In sum, I am not saying that you have to stop using software apps. In many cases, they are very necessary and useful, but you need to be careful when using them to avoid being manipulated.

Crime and Punishment: My Opinion

Beyond Crime and Punishment

📅 April 12, 2026

Why do people commit crime? Is it because they are bad, or is there a deeper reason? I personally think that nobody is born bad, but life sometimes pushes people down that path. That’s why many criminals come from difficult or poor backgrounds.

The problem is, once a person commits a crime, what should we do with them? As I said, I believe that anyone could become a criminal if life pushes them that way. But does that mean we shouldn’t punish them for their actions?

Most people see prisons as a punishment, as a way to take revenge on people who have hurt others, and I think that is a problem. We should see prisons as a place to keep people who may be a danger to others, but also as a place to understand why they ended up there, so we can prevent it in the future.

Seeing prisons only as punishment gives us the idea that there are simply good and bad people, and that bad people must be punished. For me, it is more complicated than that. Even though I agree that actions must have consequences, we also need to understand that, with better education and a healthier environment, many criminals could have become different people. Otherwise, our prisons will always be full.
